Here are some of the interesting graphs on Poverty:
Interative map showing poverty across the country
Source: Our World in Data
The visual above is an interactive data visualization tool called "Poverty Explorer" from Our World in Data. It allows users to explore poverty rates in various countries based on different poverty lines and data sources. The tool displays a world map with highlighted countries and shows the percentage of the population living in poverty in each country based on a poverty line of $2.15 per day. Users can adjust the poverty line and data source to compare poverty rates across countries, visualize changes over time, and see how poverty rates vary.
Graph showing number of people in or not in extreme poverty over the years
Source: Our World in Data
The visual above is an interactive data visualization tool from Our World in Data that displays the world population in extreme poverty over time. The tool shows a graph that depicts the number of people living in extreme poverty (defined as living on less than $1.90 per day) from the year 1820 to 2015. The graph reveals a decline in the number of people living in extreme poverty over time, with a significant reduction in poverty levels from the 1990s to 2015. The tool allows users to adjust the time frame of the graph and download the data as needed.
