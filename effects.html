Effects

The effects of poverty are widespread and can have a profound impact on individuals, families, and communities.

Here are some of the effects of poverty:

Malnutrition

People who live in poverty often do not have access to enough food or nutritious food, and this can lead to stunted growth, weakened immune systems, and other health problems.

Healthcare

Poverty can also limit access to healthcare, which can result in untreated illnesses and preventable deaths. In addition, poverty can contribute to poor living conditions, which can increase the risk of infectious diseases and other health problems.


Education

Poverty can also limit educational opportunities, which can have long-term effects on individuals and communities. Children who grow up in poverty are less likely to attend school, and those who do attend may struggle to keep up with their peers due to a lack of resources and support. This can limit their future prospects and perpetuate the cycle of poverty.

Physological effect

Poverty can have social and psychological effects, such as stigma, discrimination, and feelings of hopelessness. People who live in poverty may be marginalized and excluded from society, which can limit their ability to participate fully and achieve their potential.
